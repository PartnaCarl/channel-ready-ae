Why This Is the Priority Build

Module 10 proved the curriculum works in retail. This module proves it travels.

A startup buyer told us: "I need to see at least one module from a non-retail, non-CDW source before I trust the framework works for my cybersecurity team." This module is the answer — anchored in Natalie's Expel partner launch, validated against Carl's HPE activation model.

The 4-Step Activation Model is the sequence that turns a signed agreement into actual co-sell revenue within 90 days. It works the same whether you're activating a CDW relationship for POS hardware or an Optiv relationship for cybersecurity — because the underlying partner dynamics are identical: reps need to be found, trained, aligned on targets, and incentivized to act.

Step 1 — Relationship Building

Days 0–15. Identify 5–8 target reps. Build the account overlap list. Map the partner org. Schedule intro calls with named accounts in hand.

Step 2 — Enablement

Days 15–30. First training call. Sales play deck distributed. Battle card in every inbox. SPIF announced at end of training.

Step 3 — Alignment

Days 30–60. Recurring cadences locked. Account lists finalized. First deal regs submitted. The cord identified for this quarter.

Step 4 — Incentive

Days 30–90. SPIF program live and reinforced at every touchpoint. First QBR prep underway. First deal in late-stage or closed.

Execution

The complete 90-day activation checklist.

Days 0–15 — Relationship Building
Identify 5–8 target partner reps by territory and vertical fit
Not every rep is your rep. Pick based on territory overlap and vertical expertise.
Build account overlap list: your pipeline × their territory
Pre-populate Spreadsheet 1 so you walk into the first meeting with value, not asks.
Map partner org chart: VP → DM → SM → AM
Names, titles, LinkedIn. You need contacts at every level.
Schedule intro calls with each target rep — bring 3 account names to each
Accounts are currency. Never show up to a partner meeting empty-handed.
Days 15–30 — Enablement
Run first AM webinar / product training (30 min max)
Product overview + "reason to call" customer profiles + qualification signals. Keep it actionable.
Distribute sales play deck and battle card same day as training
Reps forget 80% within 48 hours. The assets are the memory device.
Announce SPIF program at end of training call (not the beginning)
Starting with money signals the content isn't worth their time. Ending with it rewards attendance.
Set up deal reg portal access for all trained reps
BLOCKING. A trained rep who can't submit a deal reg is a wasted touchpoint.
Days 30–60 — Alignment
Lock in recurring meeting cadences: weekly co-sell, monthly DM, quarterly QBR
Calendar invites sent AND accepted. Recurring slots are harder to cancel than to set.
Run first co-sell session: 10 named accounts, assigned ownership, next-step dates
Quality over quantity. 10 accounts with real next steps beats 50 accounts with none.
Submit first deal registrations from co-sell findings
Even early-stage deals. Deal reg protects margin and creates accountability.
Send first Win Wire to partner leadership
Accounts confirmed, calls completed, pipeline identified, what's next. Two paragraphs.
Days 60–90 — Incentive & Proof
Track SPIF payments against submissions — are reps responding to incentives?
If SPIF participation is low, the amounts may be wrong or communication failed.
Run technical deep-dive for partner SEs and architects
Month 2 enablement. The SEs validate your product technically — AMs trust their judgment.
Prepare QBR package for Day 90 review
Start building 2 weeks before. Don't scramble the night before the meeting.
First deal in late-stage or closed — the proof of activation
If no deal has progressed by Day 90, the activation has a problem. Diagnose in the QBR.

The Critical Window

90 days. Four steps. No exceptions.

Partnerships that don't achieve meaningful co-sell activity within 90 days of signing rarely recover without a significant reset. This timeline is the operating plan that prevents that outcome.

0–15DAYS

Relationship Building

Identify 5–8 target reps. Build account overlap list. Map partner org. Schedule intro calls with 3 named accounts each. Pre-populate the shared pipeline tracker. Do not proceed to enablement until you have named contacts at AM, SM, and DM level.

15–30DAYS

Enablement Launch

30-minute AM webinar. Sales play deck and battle card distributed same day. SPIF announced at end of call. Deal reg portal access provisioned to all trained reps. The rep leaves knowing: what to sell, who to sell it to, and what they get for doing it.

30–60DAYS

Alignment & First Pipeline

Weekly co-sell sessions running. Monthly DM sync established. First deal regs submitted. Win Wire #1 sent. The cord identified for this quarter. Technical deep-dive run for SEs. Cadence is now locked — both sides showing up consistently.

60–90DAYS

Proof of Concept

First deal in late-stage or closed. SPIF participation tracked and evaluated. QBR package being built. If no deal has progressed, diagnose: product fit, rep engagement, or territory mismatch? The Day 90 QBR is the first real test.
