Why This Module Exists

Your partner doesn't care about your roadmap. They care about theirs.

Carl's original note: "Remain relevant every quarter by showing the cord between our portfolio and CDW's quarterly focus." Natalie applied the same principle at Okta, mapping identity solutions to CDW's quarterly security themes.

Every quarter, the partner organization sets new priorities — a vertical push, a technology theme, a competitive battle. Partners prioritize vendors who make themselves relevant to what the partner is already selling. The "cord" is the direct connection between your product and their current initiative. Find it, show it early, and pull it every quarter.

THE PRINCIPLE
Partners don't change direction for vendors. Vendors must change framing for partners. If CDW is pushing managed security this quarter, you don't pitch your product — you pitch how your product makes their managed security offering better. Same product, different frame. That's the cord.

Retail Example

CDW Q2 push: self-checkout modernization. Cord: "Our POS platform eliminates 40% of shrink in SCO lanes — here's 3 customer profiles to target."

Cybersecurity Example

Optiv Q3 initiative: managed detection & response. Cord: "Our MDR platform plugs into existing SIEM deployments — here's the upgrade conversation."

Identity Example

CDW security team pushing zero trust. Cord: "Identity is the first pillar of zero trust — here's the 5 signals a customer is ready for IAM."

The Process

Four steps to finding and showing the cord every quarter.

🔍
Step 1 — Research the Focus
  • Read the partner's quarterly earnings call transcript Source
  • Check partner portal announcements and campaign pages Source
  • Ask your AM directly: "What's the big push this quarter?" Direct
  • Look at their job postings — hiring patterns reveal strategic bets Signal
  • Review their recent press releases and event sponsorships Signal
🔗
Step 2 — Map the Overlap
  • Write one sentence: "[Partner] is focused on [X]. We connect because [Y]." Exercise
  • If you can't write it, you don't have a cord this quarter Test
  • No cord = maintenance quarter, not growth quarter — plan accordingly Reality
  • Force the exercise even when it feels like a stretch — creativity wins Discipline
📣
Step 3 — Communicate It
  • Lead every co-sell session with the cord framing Cadence
  • "I know you're pushing [X] this quarter — here's how we fit for 3 account types" Script
  • Update your battle card to reflect the quarterly cord Assets
  • Tell the DM the cord in your Win Wire — they'll repeat it to the VP Ladder
🔄
Step 4 — Refresh Every Quarter
  • The cord expires — Q1's connection is not Q2's connection Timing
  • Set a calendar reminder 2 weeks before each quarter starts Process
  • Research new focus, write new cord sentence, update battle card Deliverable
  • Even if the focus hasn't changed, refresh messaging with new data Discipline
Execution

Quarterly cord refresh checklist.

Complete this checklist 2 weeks before each new quarter starts. It takes 2 hours and determines whether you're relevant or invisible for the next 90 days.
Research (Week -2)
Read the partner's latest earnings call transcript or quarterly update
Look for: named verticals, technology themes, competitive battles, growth targets.
Check partner portal for new campaign pages or incentive programs
New SPIF programs signal where the partner is investing attention.
Ask your AM: "What's the team focused on next quarter?"
The direct ask often surfaces priorities that aren't in press releases.
Write your cord sentence: "[Partner] is focused on [X]. We connect because [Y]."

Templates

The quarterly cord email.

Template — Quarterly Cord Update to Partner AMs

To: [Partner AM Team]
Subject: Q[X] play — how [Your Product] maps to [Partner's Focus]
Hi team, Quick note ahead of Q[X] — I want to make sure you know exactly how [Your Product] maps to what your team is focused on this quarter. YOUR Q[X] FOCUS: [Partner's initiative — e.g., "managed security" or "cloud migration" or "self-checkout modernization"] HOW WE FIT → [1-sentence connection between your product and their focus] → [Customer type 1 who's a fit — e.g., "Retailers with 50+ locations still on legacy POS"] → [Customer type 2 — e.g., "Banks upgrading their branch technology stack"] → [Customer type 3 — e.g., "Healthcare orgs under compliance pressure for endpoint security"] WHAT'S IN IT FOR YOU → Deal reg: [X]% margin protection + $[X] SPIF per registration → Services attach: your team can sell [specific services] alongside every deal → Average deal size: $[X]K including services I'll cover this in our next co-sell session on [date]. If you have a customer in mind already, reply and I'll prep a custom pitch for the call. [Your Name]

Template — Cord Summary for Win Wire to DM

To: [Partner DM]
Subject: [Your Company] × [Partner] — Q[X] alignment + pipeline update
Hi [Name], Q[X] alignment: your team is focused on [initiative]. We map directly to that through [1-sentence cord]. This quarter's plan: → [X] target accounts identified in your territory → Training session for your AM team on [date] focused specifically on [initiative] use cases → SPIF program active: $[X] per deal reg, $[X] per customer meeting Current pipeline: $[X] across [X] accounts, [X] deal regs submitted. More detail in the shared tracker. See you at our monthly sync on [date]. [Your Name]
