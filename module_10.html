Why This Module Exists

Partners don't generate pipeline by accident. They do it when you build a joint prospecting motion with them.

The difference between a partner who passes you one deal per quarter and a partner who generates consistent new pipeline is almost always whether the AE has built a structured joint prospecting habit with them. This module shows you how.

Most channel AEs wait for partners to bring them opportunities. The partner has 200 vendor relationships — if you're waiting passively, you're at the back of the line. The AEs who get consistent partner-sourced pipeline are the ones who actively build a joint prospecting motion: a regular cadence of prospecting activity where both parties are identifying accounts, reaching out together, and jointly qualifying before anyone invests time in a formal deal cycle.

Sandler's prospecting principles apply here the same way they apply to direct sales: identify the pain pattern, target the accounts where that pain pattern is likely to exist, contact the right person with the right message, and qualify fast. The difference is that in channel, you're doing this in partnership — and you need to structure the partnership so the prospecting effort is mutual, not one-sided.

THE CORE INSIGHT
Partners who prospect with you are partners who are invested in your success. Joint prospecting is the activity that converts a transactional partner relationship into a genuine co-sell partnership — and it's the fastest way to find out which partners are actually motivated.

The Joint Prospecting System

Three components that turn partners into an outbound engine.

📋
Component 1 — Target Account List
  • Build a shared list of 10–20 named target accounts Required
  • Each account must fit your ICP and the partner's book Required
  • Partner must have a relationship or access to each account Required
  • Each account gets a tier: hot, warm, cold Required
  • Reviewed and updated at every monthly sync Cadence
📅
Component 2 — Weekly Co-Prospect Call
  • 30-min weekly call: what went out, what came back Required
  • Partner owns the relationship intro, you own the qualification Role Split
  • UFC for each call — purpose and outcome stated upfront Required
  • No call without a specific action item from the previous call Required
  • Track: outreach sent, responses, meetings booked Metrics
✉
Component 3 — Co-Sourced Outreach
  • Partner sends intro, you provide the email/script Model
  • Message anchors on the end customer's likely pain — not your product Required
  • Call to action: a brief conversation, not a demo Required
  • Follow-up sequence owned by partner, supported by you Model
  • All responses routed to weekly co-prospect call Cadence

The Pain-First Outreach Message

CO-SOURCED OUTREACH TEMPLATE
From: [Partner Rep Name]
To: [Target Contact]

"I've been working with [your company's] team on some situations in [vertical] where [specific pain pattern] is creating real pressure for operations and IT leadership. A few of my customers have run into this — specifically around [two-sentence problem description]. I thought it might be worth a quick 15-minute call to see if this is something you're dealing with, or not. Are you open to a brief conversation?"

The message leads with pain, not product. The partner's credibility makes it land.
Building the Joint Prospecting Motion

From account list to first conversation — step by step.

Step 1 — The Account Planning Session

Schedule a 60-minute account planning session with the partner. Purpose: build the target account list together. Bring a proposed list of accounts based on your ICP. Ask the partner to bring their top accounts in your target segment. Combine and tier the list. Assign ownership. This session typically surfaces 15-20 real targets in the first meeting.

Step 2 — Mapping the Pain Pattern

Before you reach out to any target account, agree on the pain pattern. What problem do these accounts likely have? Why would they be feeling it now? What is the cost of not solving it?
PAIN PATTERN MAPPING WITH THE PARTNER
"Walk me through the last customer you sold this kind of solution to. What was their situation? What were they dealing with that made them ready to buy? What was the thing that finally triggered the decision?" Use the partner's real experience to build the outreach narrative. It's more compelling and more accurate than anything you could invent.

Step 3 — Launching the Outreach

Write the first outreach email together in the account planning session. Get the partner's voice into it — it should sound like them, not like a vendor template. Have the partner review it before it goes. Launch it to the top 5 accounts on the list. Review responses at the weekly co-prospect call.

Qualifying Inbound Partner Referrals

When a partner brings you an inbound referral, apply the Sandler Pain Funnel immediately — not to the end customer, but to the partner. "Before I reach out to this customer, help me understand what you know about their situation. What's the problem they're dealing with? How long have they had it? Why are they looking now?" If the partner can't answer these questions, the referral isn't qualified enough to pursue yet.

From the Field

What separates partners who generate pipeline from partners who pass leads.

The Difference Between a Referral and a Lead

A referral is: "You should talk to [Company] — they might need something like this." A lead is: "I talked to [Name] at [Company] last week. They're dealing with [specific pain], they've been trying to solve it for six months, they have budget, and [Name] said they'd take a call from you this week." The joint prospecting motion produces leads, not referrals. Referrals require you to restart the qualification process. Leads are already partway through it.

Maintaining the Weekly Cadence

CADENCE RULE
The weekly co-prospect call is the most important meeting in a productive partner relationship. It is more important than the QBR. It is the place where pipeline actually gets created. Protect it. If a partner cancels more than twice in a row with no reschedule, apply NRS (Module 06). A partner who won't show up to the co-prospect call is a partner who isn't building pipeline with you — and that's a qualification failure, not a scheduling conflict.

When the Partner's Network Is Tapped Out

After 60-90 days of joint prospecting with the same partner, you may find you've exhausted the easy targets in their current book. This is the signal to discuss expansion: new verticals, new geographies, new account segments. The joint prospecting motion that built the first tier of pipeline is also the template for building the next one. But it requires a fresh account planning session and a new target list.
