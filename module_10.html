∨Channel Activation System™ — Pain Funnel · Joint Prospecting

Partner-sourced pipeline doesn't appear on its own. You have to prospect to create it.

The assumption that partners will proactively bring you deals is one of the most expensive beliefs in channel sales. Partners have dozens of vendors competing for their attention. Without a structured prospecting motion — one that makes it easy for partners to identify opportunities and brings you into the conversation early — the pipeline you get from partners is whatever they happen to think of when a deal randomly surfaces.

Joint prospecting flips that equation. Instead of waiting for partners to bring you deals, you proactively work their account base to surface opportunities that neither of you would have identified alone. The partner brings the customer relationship. You bring the Pain Funnel diagnostic. Together, you find problems that your product solves — before the customer has started a formal evaluation, before they've issued an RFP, before a competitor has gotten there first.

The Core Insight

The best co-sell deals don't come from partners responding to customer requests — they come from joint prospecting that uncovers customer pain before the customer has formalized it as an initiative. That's where you win before the competition starts.

The joint prospecting framework — from account selection through first call.

Step 1 — Account Selection

Work with your partner to identify 10–20 customers in their existing base who match the profile of your best-fit buyers. The selection criteria should be specific: industry vertical, company size, technology stack, and ideally a known pain signal (recent compliance requirement, a public announcement about digital transformation, a competitor win they lost). Vague account selection produces vague conversations.

Account Selection Conversation With Partner

"I want to work your book of business with you — not wait for deals to come to us. Let's build a list of 15 accounts in your base that look like our best-fit customers. I'll tell you the profile, you tell me which of your accounts fit it, and we'll prioritize the top 10 to call together this month. Can we set aside 30 minutes this week to do that mapping?"

Step 2 — Pre-Call Preparation

Before any joint prospecting call, prepare the partner on how to open the conversation. The partner leads with the customer relationship. You lead with the Pain Funnel. The roles should be clear before the call starts, not negotiated on it.

Step 3 — The Joint Prospecting Call

Joint Prospecting Call Structure

Partner opens (2 min): "I wanted to bring [vendor AE] in today because we've been seeing a lot of companies like yours dealing with [specific challenge] — and I wanted to understand whether that's something you're experiencing."

You run the Pain Funnel (15 min): Start with the surface problem, move to business impact, listen for Level 3 pain. Let the customer do the talking.

Both align on next step (5 min): Set a UFC for the next conversation before you hang up.

Getting partners to open their accounts to you — and keeping that access.

Partners are protective of their customer relationships — and for good reason. Every vendor AE who has come before you has made promises about not selling around them, not pulling deals direct, and not using their customer introductions to circumvent the partnership. Many of those promises have been broken. The access you get to a partner's account base is earned, not given.

Earning Account Access

What Earns Access

Demonstrating you make them better

When a partner sees that your joint prospecting produces deals they couldn't have found alone, they open more accounts. Every successful joint call is an investment that pays interest in future access. Document the outcomes and reference them explicitly.

What Loses Access

Bypassing the partner

Going direct to the customer without the partner's knowledge — for any reason — destroys the prospecting relationship permanently. If a customer reaches out to you directly on a partner account, the first call you make is to the partner, not back to the customer.

Field notes: The prospecting behaviors that compound over time.

Joint Prospecting as a QBR Input

Every joint prospecting session should produce a named list of accounts with the pain signals identified and the next action agreed. This list becomes your QBR input: "Here are the accounts we prospected last quarter, here's what we found, here's what closed, and here's what's still in motion." This transforms the QBR from a retrospective into a forward-looking prospecting review — and gives the partner a reason to prioritize the next joint session.

Scaling the Motion

Once a joint prospecting rhythm is established with one rep at a partner, it becomes a playbook for the entire team. Document what's working — the account selection criteria, the call structure, the pain signals that convert — and share it with the partner's manager. Successful joint prospecting with one rep is the proof point for expanding the motion to the entire territory.
