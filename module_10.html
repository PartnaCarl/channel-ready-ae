Why This Moment Matters

The contract is not the win. The first 90 days are.

Most startup AEs treat a signed partner agreement as the finish line. It's the starting gun. The VAR's sales reps don't know you exist yet. The relationship needs to be activated — deliberately, structurally, and fast — before your window closes.

A signed CDW agreement means CDW's leadership said yes. It does not mean CDW's account managers, inside reps, or field sellers know what to sell, who to call, or why your product matters to their customers. That is your job for the next 90 days.

THE CORE INSIGHT
You have approximately 6–8 weeks before a new partner relationship goes cold without intentional activation. CDW's sellers have 200+ vendor relationships competing for their attention. You are not special yet. You become special by being the easiest, most supportive, most lucrative vendor to sell. That reputation is built in the first 90 days — or not at all.

Kickoff Checklist

Everything you must do in the first 30 days.

This checklist is built from real partnership launches. Items marked "blocking" mean the partnership cannot progress without them. Items marked "accelerating" are what separate average launches from fast ones.
Week 1 — Administrative Foundation
Confirm all agreements executed and filed (partner agreement, W9, supplier setup)
BLOCKING. No SPIF payments, no MDF access, no deal registration eligibility until this is complete.
Obtain deal registration portal access for partner reps
BLOCKING. Partner reps cannot submit deal regs until they have credentials. Set this up before the first training call.
Map your internal contacts to partner org chart: VP → Director → Manager → rep level
ACCELERATING. You need a named contact at each level before you can operate the heatmap.
Set up shared pipeline tracking spreadsheet (two columns: your pipeline, partner's view)
ACCELERATING. "Same info reporting both ways" is the principle. The spreadsheet exists from Day 1 — even if it's empty.
Establish recurring meeting cadences: weekly co-sell call, monthly DM sync, quarterly QBR
BLOCKING. Calendar invites sent and accepted before the first activation week. Recurring slots are harder to cancel than to set.
Week 2 — Enablement Launch
Run first AM webinar / kickoff training (your product overview + pitch for their sellers)
BLOCKING. Partner reps cannot sell what they don't know. The first training is the first impression — keep it under 30 minutes, end with a "reason to call" list of 5 account types.
Deliver the sales play deck to partner AM contacts (not a product brochure — a "here's how to sell it" guide)
ACCELERATING. The CDW Sales Plays format: tier signals, anchor products, services pull-through, FAQs, email scripts. Give reps what they need to act independently.
Activate seller incentive program — communicate SPIF structure to every rep who attended training
ACCELERATING. A rep who doesn't know about the $500 deal reg SPIF will not think about deal registration. Tell them immediately after the first training call.
Distribute battle card / one-pager: 1 page, max, with "REFRESH IF / MODERNIZE IF / TRANSFORM IF" qualification signals
ACCELERATING. Reps do not re-read decks. They re-read battle cards. One page, in their inbox, with the signals that tell them when to call you.
Week 3–4 — Co-Sell Activation
Build Spreadsheet 1: your active pipeline where the partner has relationships
BLOCKING for co-sell. You cannot run a co-sell motion without a shared target account list. Pull your pipeline, map to partner's accounts, flag mutual acquisition targets.
Build Spreadsheet 2: your unattached prospects with no partner assigned, mapped to partner AMs
ACCELERATING. These are your net-new-logo opportunities. Give the partner rep a list of accounts in their territory that you want their help opening.
Run first co-sell session: review both spreadsheets, assign ownership, set next-step dates per account
BLOCKING. The co-sell session is the engine. Without a structured review of named accounts, the relationship produces goodwill but not pipeline.
Submit first deal registrations from co-sell session findings
ACCELERATING. The fastest way to demonstrate value to a partner rep is a deal reg that protects their margin on an account they brought you. Do this in the first 30 days.
Send first Win Wire — even if it's a pipeline update, not a closed deal
ACCELERATING. A Win Wire after the first 30 days signals professional discipline and keeps your partner's management aware of progress. Format: accounts confirmed, calls completed, pipeline identified, what's next.

The Activation Framework

Every successful VAR partnership runs on four pillars.

This framework comes directly from Carl's CDW-Toshiba Plan on a Page — but it applies to any vendor-VAR relationship regardless of product. A startup AE managing their first partner needs all four running simultaneously within 60 days of signature.

🎓
Pillar 1 — Enablement
  • AM-wide webinar — product overview and pitch for generalist reps Month 1
  • Deep-dive technical training — for partner SEs and solution architects Month 2
  • Mini-enablement embedded in every co-sell session Ongoing
  • Sales play deck distributed to all attending reps Month 1
  • Battle card / one-pager for in-field use Month 1

Startup AE version: you cannot afford a dedicated PSR. You do the mini-enablement yourself at the start of every co-sell call. Keep it under 5 minutes, keep it tied to one specific "reason to call" for that week.

📣
Pillar 2 — Marketing
  • Co-branded email campaign to partner's customer base Month 2–3
  • ROI one-pager co-branded for partner AM use Month 1
  • Seller incentive program — SPIF ladder by action type Month 1
  • Executive access events (NRF, Groceryshop, etc.) Quarterly
  • MDF request submitted for campaign activities Month 2

Startup AE version: Start with the SPIF ladder — it costs almost nothing and immediately gives partner reps a reason to act. Co-branded materials and MDF come once you have the first deal in pipeline.

🤝
Pillar 3 — Co-Selling
  • Spreadsheet 1: active pipeline with partner relationship flags Week 3
  • Spreadsheet 2: unattached prospects mapped to partner AMs Week 3
  • Weekly co-sell session reviewing both lists Ongoing
  • Mini-enablement segment in every session Ongoing
  • Deal registration submitted per co-sell outcome Ongoing

"Same info reporting both ways" is the operating principle. What you see in the pipeline, they see. What they close, you track. No information asymmetry in a co-sell relationship.

🎯
Pillar 4 — Events
  • Industry event co-attendance (your vertical's flagship event) Annual
  • Private executive dinner co-hosted with partner Annual
  • Joint customer reception at major event Annual
  • Entry requirement: 30-min briefing from partner AMs Per event
  • Partner AM makes the customer ask, you support Per event

Startup AE version: You may not have event budget in Year 1. Start by getting on your partner's event calendar as a supporting resource — not the host. Show up, support, then earn hosting rights in Year 2.

THE CORD PRINCIPLE
Every quarter, the partner has a new focus area. Your job is to show "the cord" — the direct connection between their quarterly initiative and your product. Storage quarter? Here's how we fit. Security push? Here's our adjacent play. Partners prioritize vendors who make themselves relevant to what the partner is already selling — not vendors who ask the partner to change direction. Find the cord. Show it early. Pull it every quarter.

QUALIFICATION SIGNALS
REFRESH IF: POS devices over 5 years old, or breaking into a new account.
MODERNIZE IF: Shrink, self-checkout fraud, or multi-store inconsistency challenges.
TRANSFORM IF: Executive-led digital strategy with multi-year budget authority.

The most important FAQ in the CDW Sales Plays deck: "What if I've never sold retail before?" — Answer: Start with REFRESH as an infrastructure conversation. You are not expected to be a retail expert. Overlays will support you. Your role is to open a discovery conversation, not close a platform deal on the first call.

How to Build Your Own Play Structure

Step 1 — Define Your Tiers

What are the 3 versions of a deal with your product? Entry (hardware/software only), Mid (+ services), Full (+ strategic platform)? Name them simply. Refresh/Modernize/Transform works. So does Basic/Growth/Enterprise.

Step 2 — Identify the Signals

What does the customer say or show when they're in each tier? Write the qualification signals in plain language. "REFRESH IF..." is a format any rep can memorize in 60 seconds.

Step 3 — Map the Pull-Through

What services does your VAR want to sell alongside each tier? That's their reason to push your deal. No pull-through = no motivation. Build the services map before you run your first training.

You don't ask partner reps to act. You pay them to act.

Most startup AEs don't know this is how the channel economy works. CDW's reps have 200+ vendor relationships. They act on the ones that reward specific behaviors with specific dollar amounts. The incentive ladder is not optional — it is the mechanism that creates repeatable partner rep behavior.

How to Build Your Incentive Ladder on a Startup Budget

If you have $5,000 to start

Allocate: $25/training (up to 50 reps = $1,250). $100/demo (up to 20 = $2,000). $300/deal reg (up to 5 = $1,500). Reserve $250 as buffer. You've now created a structured incentive program for 75 rep interactions.

If you have $1,000 to start

Skip training incentive. Focus: $200/customer meeting (up to 3 = $600) + $100/demo (up to 4 = $400). Fewer incentives, higher threshold, still creates action. Tell reps upfront: "First 3 customer meetings get $200 each."

Non-cash incentives that work

Amazon gift cards, Airpods, Yeti coolers, dinner reservations, event access. Partner reps respond to tangible, fast-to-deliver rewards. A $75 Amazon card in 48 hours beats a $200 "will process in 60 days" check.

The rule nobody tells you

Always announce the incentive at the end of the training call, not the beginning. Starting with "you'll get $25 for this" signals the content isn't worth their time. Ending with "and for showing up today..." rewards action already taken.

The Critical Window

90 days. Three phases. No exceptions.

Partnerships that don't achieve meaningful co-sell activity within 90 days of signing rarely recover without significant reset. This timeline is the operational plan that prevents that outcome.

1–2WEEKS

Administrative Foundation

All agreements executed. Deal reg portal access provisioned. Internal/external org map built. Shared pipeline tracker created (empty is fine). Recurring calendar slots sent and accepted. Do not proceed to enablement until these are done — a rep who attends a training call but can't submit a deal reg is a wasted touchpoint.

2–3WEEKS

First Training + Incentive Activation

AM-wide webinar: 30 minutes max. Product overview, one "reason to call" customer profile, three qualification signals. Sales play deck distributed same day. SPIF structure announced at the end of the call. Battle card in every rep's inbox within 24 hours. The rep should leave this call knowing: what to sell, who to sell it to, and what they get for doing it.

3–4WEEKS

First Co-Sell Session

Build Spreadsheet 1 (your pipeline with partner relationships) and Spreadsheet 2 (unattached prospects mapped to partner AMs). Run the first co-sell session: review both lists, assign ownership per account, set next-step dates. Goal for this session: 10 accounts with a named rep and a next action. Not 100. Ten. Quality over quantity on the first pass.

30DAYS

First Deal Registrations + Win Wire

Submit first deal registrations from co-sell session outcomes. Even if the deals are early-stage — deal reg protects margin and creates accountability. Send the first Win Wire to the partner's leadership: accounts confirmed, training completed, co-selling calls run, pipeline identified, next steps. Two paragraphs. This is what keeps the relationship on leadership's radar.

30–60DAYS

Cadence Locked In, Deeper Enablement

Weekly co-sell sessions are running and producing named account action items. Monthly DM sync is happening. Technical deep-dive training run for SEs and solution architects. MDF request submitted if applicable. The "cord" — connection between your product and the partner's Q2 focus — identified and communicated to all reps.

60–90DAYS

First Revenue Signal + QBR Prep

First deal in late-stage or closed. If not closed, first commit-level opportunity with a specific close date. Pipeline summary updated. QBR package being built for the 90-day mark: what was committed, what happened, what changed, what's the 90-day forward plan. The QBR at Day 90 is the first real test of whether the partnership is working — prepare for it from Day 1.

90DAYS

First QBR — The Proof of Concept

Agenda: State of Business (your side), State of Business (their side), 90-day review vs. commitments, pipeline by tier (Refresh/Modernize/Transform or your equivalent), top 10 active accounts with status, challenges and corrective actions, Q2 joint plan. The QBR at Day 90 does not need perfect numbers. It needs to demonstrate that you are a professional, data-driven vendor partner who shows up with receipts — not excuses.
