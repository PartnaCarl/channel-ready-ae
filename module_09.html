Why This Module Exists

A QBR with data — even bad data — beats a QBR with promises.

The QBR does not need perfect numbers. It needs to demonstrate that you are a professional, data-driven vendor partner who shows up with receipts — not excuses. This module teaches the both-sides agenda: what you present as a vendor AND what the partner DM is actually evaluating in that room.

NATALIE'S PERSPECTIVE — FROM THE PARTNER CHAIR
What the DM is evaluating: Is this vendor producing revenue? Are my reps engaged? Is the vendor showing up consistently? Is the data clean? Is this worth another quarter of my team's time? A vendor who shows up with actual numbers — even bad ones — gets more respect than one with a deck full of aspirations.
The Structure

The 7-part QBR agenda — vendor delivery + partner evaluation.

Your Delivery (Vendor Side)

1. State of Business: Pipeline, deal reg count, revenue, year-over-year
2. Top 10 Active Accounts: Status, next steps, where you need help
3. What's Working: Wins, successful co-sells, rep engagement
4. What Needs to Change: Stalled deals, process friction
5. Forward Plan: Next quarter targets, new plays, enablement calendar
6. The Cord: How you connect to next quarter's partner focus
7. The Ask: What specific support do you need from the partner org?

Partner Evaluation (DM Perspective)

The DM is scoring you on 5 dimensions:

Revenue: Did this vendor produce enough to justify the time investment?
Engagement: Are my reps actually working with this vendor consistently?
Professionalism: Does this vendor bring data, or do they bring excuses?
Trajectory: Is the trend improving, flat, or declining?
Effort-to-Value: Is this partnership worth maintaining vs. replacing with a different vendor?

The Hard Part

How to run a QBR when you have 2 deals and no data.

Your first QBR will have thin numbers. That's expected. The goal is not to impress with volume — it's to demonstrate discipline.

What to Show

The accounts you've reviewed together. The actions taken since contract. What you learned about where your product fits. A specific plan for the next 90 days with named accounts and named reps. Completion rates on your Six Asks scorecard.

What to Avoid

Vague optimism ("we think pipeline is building"). Blame ("your reps aren't engaging"). Excuses ("the market is tough"). Massive slide decks to compensate for thin data. Asking "what can we do better?" without proposing an answer first.

Thin data + clear plan beats no data + vague optimism. Every time. The DM has sat through 50 QBRs from vendors with no data and big promises. Be the vendor who has a spreadsheet, even if it's half-empty, and a specific plan for what fills the other half.
