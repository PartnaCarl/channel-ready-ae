Executive Summary

The one-page version.

Channel Ready AE is a B2B sales training company offering a TRACE-based training program specifically adapted for channel Account Executives. The program teaches quota-carrying Account Executives at technology companies how to apply The Multiplier Method — Partner Fit Filter, Co-Sell Alignment Agreements, Negative Reverse Selling, DISC, Identity/Role Theory, and No-Free-Consulting — to the indirect channel, where the buyer is a partner rep, not an end customer.

The curriculum is built from practitioner IP: Carl Clendenin's 10+ years running the enterprise VAR ecosystem at Toshiba (CDW, SHI, Insight, WWT, and seven more national VARs), and Natalie Clendenin's channel AE experience at Expel, LastPass (4× CDW revenue growth), Okta, and Varonis. All 12 modules are complete and production-ready. Every module has a participant workbook, a facilitator slide deck with speaker notes, a HeyGen AI avatar intro video script, and full module content on channelreadyae.com. The first cohort can run the day enrollment closes.

Delivery is a blended model: each session opens with a 2–3 minute HeyGen AI avatar intro video (Carl or Natalie on-screen, no scheduling required), followed by live facilitation using the phase slide deck, and closes with structured workbook time. Participants carry a binder through all three phases. The Scorecard tracks their activity across the entire program. The member portal at channelreadyae.com gives enrolled AEs gated access to all 12 module pages, reference materials, and downloadable frameworks.

The business has two revenue streams. Training: the 12-Week TRACE Cohort at $4,997/seat (employer-sponsored default), team licensing at the same per-seat rate. Consulting: Workshop as the front-door engagement ($2,500–$3,500 base, $7,500–$12,500 customized, $15K–$25K deep), Partner-Selling Readiness Assessment (PSRA) free or $1,500 paid (credited to Workshop), Manager Reinforcement Kit ($2,500 standalone or $3K–$5K/mo retainer). The PSRA is the top-of-funnel diagnostic — it maps partner motion gaps directly to Workshop scope and feeds cohort enrollment.

Proprietary Methodology

The Multiplier Method™

Most sales training assumes you own the relationship. Channel doesn't work that way.

You're influencing without control — selling through someone whose loyalty is split between you, five other vendors, and their own quota. MEDDIC doesn't teach that. Challenger doesn't teach that. Nothing does.

The Multiplier Method fills that gap. A proprietary channel methodology built from real-world execution — the first designed specifically for Account Executives who sell through partners, not just to them.

The TRACE Framework™

Five stages. One channel-capable Account Executive.

The core of The Multiplier Method is a five-stage operating model called TRACE. Each stage maps the channel sales motion from partner selection to measurable yield.

Stage Name What It Does
R Recruit Qualify the right partner for this deal and segment. Replaces the Pain Funnel, which assumes a direct buyer relationship.
E Enable Ensure the partner has everything they need to sell on your behalf. Partner-facing version of MEDDIC's Decision Criteria.
L Link Identify and develop your Champion inside the partner org — not the end customer. This is the stage nobody teaches.
A Align Confirm you and the partner are pursuing the same win. A co-sell version of the Upfront Contract.
Y Yield Measure what the partner motion actually produced — broken down by sourced, influenced, and fulfilled pipeline.

Why L — Link is the stage nobody teaches

Most Account Executives treat partner reps like customers. They pitch them. They demo to them. They follow up on them.

That's wrong.

Partner reps are internal champions. You don't sell to them — you arm them, coach them, and make them look like heroes to their customers. When you get that right, one conversation with one partner rep unlocks deals you'd never find on your own.

That's the multiplier. One Account Executive. Dozens of partner reps selling on your behalf. Each one armed, coached, and incentivized. That's the math that makes the channel model work — and it's the skill this program is built to teach.

The Problem

Channel Account Executives are trained to close direct. The indirect model requires a different system.

Technology companies at every stage route revenue through partners — Value-Added Resellers, Managed Service Providers, distributors, Independent Software Vendors. The Account Executives managing those relationships were trained in direct-sales methodology. They pitch. They give away Intellectual Property. They build business plans for partners who take the analysis to a lower-priced competitor. They run Quarterly Business Reviews that report on the past instead of committing the future.

"The channel Account Executive who gives away free consulting is the channel Account Executive who has no pipeline. The Multiplier Method fixes this — but no one has adapted it for the channel. Until now."

The Training Gap

Zero programs address the indirect channel

The Multiplier Method, MEDDIC, Challenger, and SPIN Selling all target direct-sales Account Executives. The indirect channel has a fundamentally different dynamic: the partner is not the buyer, joint sales require different qualification, and Intellectual Property protection is a survival skill.

The Cost of No-Framework

Free consulting bleeds margin to competitors

Channel Account Executives without a TRACE framework over-invest in unqualified partners, give away technical resources before commitment, and accept non-committal Quarterly Business Reviews as normal. Each free consulting engagement is margin bled directly to a competitor.

The Market Tailwind

The problem is larger in 2026 than 2022

Post-2022 VC pressure compressed channel teams at nearly every B2B tech company. Account Executives inherited the partner motion without training. 73% of IT spending flows through partners — and that percentage is growing.

No Incumbent in the Niche

Whitespace from $2,500 Workshop to $4,997/seat Cohort

Channel consulting firms (Spur Reply, JS Group, Alexander Group) target large enterprises at $50K–$200K+ minimums. Direct-sales methodology providers (Challenger, MEDDIC, SPIN, Force Management) teach selling motions that don't translate to the partner channel. The TRACE-adapted channel training niche is genuinely unoccupied at the SMB / mid-market price point.

The Product

The curriculum adapts five TRACE stages — wrapped in our proprietary The TRACE Framework™ — specifically for the indirect channel. Each module focuses on one dimension and applies it to real partner situations: Value-Added Reseller conversations, joint sales calls, Quarterly Business Reviews, partner onboarding, and competitive displacement.

Repeatable Channel Motion

TRACE-based Quarterly Business Reviews, joint prospecting through partner ecosystems, competitive intelligence using The TRACE posture, and building the operating system so The Multiplier Method becomes the default, not the exception.

When a VP Sales evaluates this program, they're comparing against four alternatives. Here's the honest case for The Multiplier Method against each one.

vs. Hire a VP of Channel

A full-time VP of Channel hire is the gold standard — but at $200K+ base plus benefits plus ramp time, it's not accessible until $30M+ Annual Recurring Revenue. Channel Ready AE delivers a shared TRACE language for your entire team in 90 days at a fraction of the cost. Our Workshop ($2.5K–$25K) and Manager Reinforcement Kit retainer ($3K–$5K/mo) bridge the gap for companies not yet ready to hire.

vs. Big Consultancy (Alexander Group, Spur Reply)

$50K–$200K minimum. Built for enterprises. Not you.

Firms like Alexander Group and Spur Reply do excellent work for Fortune 500 channel programs. Their minimum engagements start at $50K. Their Intellectual Property is generic. Their practitioners are analysts, not working channel Account Executives. We're built specifically for Series A–C companies with 3–15 Account Executives who need a methodology now, not a strategy deck in 6 months. Workshop entry at $2,500 — diagnostic-to-engagement in days, not months.

vs. General Training (Chanimal, Channel Institute)

General channel management. Not The Multiplier Method. Not your problem.

Chanimal University and Channel Institute teach channel management broadly — partner programs, co-marketing, program structure. They don't teach quota-carrying Account Executives how to apply The Multiplier Method's selling system to partner conversations. Different problem, different buyer, different outcome. We're the only Multiplier Method for channel program at any price point.
