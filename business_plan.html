Executive Summary

The one-page version.

Channel-Ready AE is a B2B sales training company that licenses a Sandler Sales methodology program specifically adapted for channel Account Executives. The program teaches quota-carrying AEs at technology companies how to apply the Sandler system — Pain Funnel, Up-Front Contracts, Negative Reverse Selling, DISC, Identity/Role Theory, and No-Free-Consulting — to the indirect channel, where the buyer is a partner rep, not an end customer.

The curriculum is built from practitioner IP: Carl Clendenin's 10+ years running the enterprise VAR ecosystem at Toshiba (CDW, SHI, Insight, WWT, and seven more national VARs), and Natalie Clendenin's channel AE experience at Expel, LastPass (4× CDW revenue growth), Okta, and Varonis. All 12 modules are complete.

The business has two revenue streams. Training: $400/seat/month cohort-based program. Consulting: Channel Strategy Audits ($2,500 flat), team workshops ($5K–$15K), and Fractional VP of Channel retainers ($8K–$12K/month). Consulting serves as top-of-funnel entry for training — the audit surfaces the gaps; the curriculum fixes them.

Why Now

The niche is unoccupied

Sandler, Challenger, MEDDIC, and SPIN Selling all teach direct sales. Zero programs exist for the indirect channel at the $400/seat price point.

The Problem

Channel AEs are trained to close direct. The indirect model requires a different system.

Technology companies at every stage route revenue through partners — VARs, MSPs, distributors, ISVs. The AEs managing those relationships were trained in direct-sales methodology. They pitch. They give away IP. They build business plans for partners who take the analysis to a lower-priced competitor. They run QBRs that report on the past instead of committing the future.

"The channel AE who gives away free consulting is the channel AE who has no pipeline. The Sandler system fixes this — but no one has adapted it for the channel. Until now."

The Training Gap

Zero programs address the indirect channel

Sandler, MEDDIC, Challenger, and SPIN Selling all target direct-sales AEs. The indirect channel has a fundamentally different dynamic: the partner is not the buyer, joint sales require different qualification, and IP protection is a survival skill.

The Cost of No-Framework

Free consulting bleeds margin to competitors

Channel AEs without a Sandler framework over-invest in unqualified partners, give away technical resources before commitment, and accept non-committal QBRs as normal. Each free consulting engagement is margin bled directly to a competitor.

The Market Tailwind

The problem is larger in 2026 than 2022

Post-2022 VC pressure compressed channel teams at nearly every B2B tech company. AEs inherited the partner motion without training. 73% of IT spending flows through partners — and that percentage is growing.

No Incumbent in the Niche

Whitespace at the $400/seat price point

Channel consulting firms (Spur Reply, JS Group, Alexander Group) target large enterprises at $50K–$200K+ minimums. Sandler's licensed partners teach direct-sales methodology. The Sandler-for-channel training niche is genuinely unoccupied.

The Product

12 modules. Six Sandler frameworks. One channel-capable AE.

The curriculum adapts six core Sandler frameworks — wrapped in our proprietary Channel Activation System™ — specifically for the indirect channel. Each module focuses on one dimension and applies it to real partner situations: VAR conversations, joint sales calls, QBRs, partner onboarding, and competitive displacement.

Phase 1 · Modules 1–4

Sandler Mindset for Channel

I/R Theory, why channel AEs fail, partner qualification using the Pain Funnel, Up-Front Contracts, and Pain Funnel applied to partner conversations. Product-agnostic. Applies identically across VAR, MSP, ISV, distributor.

Phase 2 · Modules 5–8

The Sandler Selling System in Channel

DISC for partner types, Negative Reverse Selling for stalled partners, No-Free-Consulting for IP protection, and the full anatomy of a channel deal with Sandler checkpoints at every stage.

Phase 3 · Modules 9–12

Repeatable Channel Motion

Sandler-based QBRs, joint prospecting through partner ecosystems, competitive intelligence using Sandler posture, and building the operating system so Sandler becomes the default, not the exception.

Consulting Entry Point

Channel Strategy Audit → Curriculum

A 2-hour working session ($2,500) that diagnoses the partner motion and maps findings directly to curriculum modules. Designed as the consulting-to-training pipeline. Fractional VP of Channel retainers available for teams building from scratch.

Certificate & Credential

Graduates earn the Channel-Ready AE certificate — a practitioner credential demonstrating fluency in six Sandler frameworks as applied to the indirect channel. Certificate renewal at $299/person every two years creates a recurring revenue stream. The channel training certification market has two incumbent players (Chanimal University, Channel Institute) — neither targets startup/growth-stage companies. Our certification fills that gap.

Competitive Position

The battle card. What buyers are comparing.

When a VP Sales evaluates this program, they're comparing against four alternatives. Here's the honest case for Channel-Ready AE against each one.

vs. Hire a VP of Channel

$250K+ fully loaded. 6–12 month ramp. One opinion.

A full-time VP of Channel hire is the gold standard — but at $200K+ base plus benefits plus ramp time, it's not accessible until $30M+ ARR. Channel-Ready AE delivers a shared Sandler language for your entire team in 90 days at a fraction of the cost. Our Fractional VP retainer bridges the gap for companies not yet ready to hire.

vs. Big Consultancy (Alexander Group, Spur Reply)

$50K–$200K minimum. Built for enterprises. Not you.

Firms like Alexander Group and Spur Reply do excellent work for Fortune 500 channel programs. Their minimum engagements start at $50K. Their IP is generic. Their practitioners are analysts, not working channel AEs. We're built specifically for Series A–C companies with 3–15 AEs who need a methodology now, not a strategy deck in 6 months.

vs. General Training (Chanimal, Channel Institute)

General channel management. Not Sandler. Not your problem.

Chanimal University and Channel Institute teach channel management broadly — partner programs, co-marketing, program structure. They don't teach quota-carrying AEs how to apply Sandler's selling system to partner conversations. Different problem, different buyer, different outcome. We're the only Sandler-for-channel program at any price point.
