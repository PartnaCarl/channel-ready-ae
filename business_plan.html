Executive Summary

The one-page version.

Channel-Ready AE is a B2B enablement company that licenses a 6-month training program to technology startups that want to build their first channel partner motion — without hiring a dedicated channel manager first. The program teaches founders, VPs of Sales, and account executives how to identify their first VAR partner, structure the relationship, manage deal registrations, run co-sell motions, and sustain a partner ecosystem with the bandwidth of a lean sales team.

The curriculum is built from 20+ years of real practitioner IP: actual QBRs, live joint business plans, co-sell campaign frameworks, deal registration data, incentive structures, and sales plays from Carl and Natalie's combined careers at Toshiba, Expel, HPE, CDW, LastPass, Okta, Varonis, and Juniper. All 12 modules are built and live. The AI agent add-on — 8 named agents, 24 underlying task agents, 3 unlock tiers — is in development as a separate product.

Revenue is generated through seat-based licensing ($1,200/individual, $5,000–$10,000/team) with a recurring AI agent add-on ($300–$500/seat/month) available after Month 3. Year 1 target: $108,000. Year 2 with recurring agent subscriptions: $319,000. Break-even at Month 8 from first sale.

WHY NOW
Post-2022 VC pressure pushed lean GTM structures across the industry. Channel teams were cut at nearly every mid-market B2B tech company. AEs inherited the partner motion with no training and no playbook. This program is the fix. No incumbent exists in this niche — Sandler and Challenger teach direct sales. Channel consulting firms like Spur Reply target $50K+ enterprise contracts. The startup channel training niche is unoccupied.
The Problem

Startups get approached by CDW. They have no idea what to do.

Technology companies at the seed-to-Series B stage routinely get inbound from national VARs — CDW, SHI, Insight, Softchoice. These relationships can generate meaningful revenue. But the team has no framework for responding. They don't know what a deal registration is, how VAR economics work, what a co-sell motion looks like, or how to run a QBR when they have two pipeline items and no historical data.

"The typical startup moment: a CDW rep emails your founder. Your team freezes because nobody knows what to do next. We built this program to fix that moment."

The Training Gap

Sandler, MEDDIC, Challenger, and SPIN Selling all teach direct sales. None address the co-sell and indirect channel motion now expected of AEs at lean startups. Zero programs exist that teach this specifically.

The Cost of Inaction

Partner relationships deteriorate within 90 days without intentional activation. CDW reps deprioritize vendors who can't support them. Revenue that should flow through the channel goes to a better-supported competitor.

The Market Tailwind

Post-2022 VC pressure has pushed lean GTM structures across the industry. AEs are being told to "handle channel" with no training and no playbook. The gap is live and growing.

No Incumbent in the Niche

Channel consulting firms (Spur Reply, JS Group) target large enterprises at $50K+ minimums. PRM platforms assume a channel team already exists. The startup channel training niche is genuinely unoccupied.

The Product

12 modules. 6 months. One channel-capable team.

The curriculum is practitioner-built from real source material — not case studies or theory. Every module carries contributor tags: Carl's vendor-side IP (Toshiba, HPE, Juniper) and Natalie's reseller + startup-side IP (CDW rep experience, Expel, LastPass, Okta, Varonis). All 12 modules are fully built and live.

Phase 1 — Months 1–2

Channel 101. VAR economics, partner structure, the Six Things framework, the Cord Principle, deal registration mechanics. Product-agnostic. Applies identically to retail, cybersecurity, SaaS, and infrastructure.

Phase 2 — Months 3–4

Operating the Motion. 4-Step Activation Model, co-sell campaign design (Using vs. Leveraging), incentive architecture, 3-tier sales play model, QBR execution, partnership kickoff. Every module includes a Build Your Own section.

Phase 3 — Months 5–6

Scale & Agents. KPI frameworks, pipeline reporting, Win Wire cadence, building the internal case for a channel hire. Module 12 introduces the AI agent stack — what each agent does, how to supervise it, and when to trust it.

The AI Agent Add-On — Separate Product, In Development

8 named agents, 24 underlying task agents, 3 unlock tiers. Agents are trained on the same knowledge base as the curriculum. The Co-Sell Agent — the flagship — automates the motion Natalie built manually at LastPass that produced 4× CDW revenue growth in 12 months. Available to program graduates after Month 3. Does not gate the training launch.

Core Tier — Month 3

Compliance Agent (onboarding, contracts, tiering) · Enablement Agent (training, collateral, certification) · Marketing Agent (MDF, SPIFs, competitive intel)

Growth Tier — Month 6

Co-Sell Agent ★ · Deal Agent (deal reg lifecycle) · Communication Agent (email, Win Wire) · Reporting Agent (QBR prep, dashboards)

Scale Tier — Year 2+

Events Agent (executive dinners, joint customer hospitality). Relevant once budget and multiple active partners exist.

Risk Analysis

What could go wrong — and how we respond.

RISKLIKELIHOODIMPACTMITIGATION
Non-compete violation MEDIUM HIGH Attorney reviews both employment agreements before any customer contact. Operate under separate LLC. No pilot customer contacted until legal clears. Budget: $1,500–$2,500.
Market awareness — nobody searches for this HIGH MEDIUM Outbound-first GTM. LinkedIn content engine (3×/week each founder) creates demand pull. Channel Readiness Audit tool generates SEO inbound. Category creation is the explicit goal.
Sales cycle longer than projected MEDIUM MEDIUM Warm network first 3 months — no cold outbound until curriculum is live. Pilot pricing ($5K vs $7.5K) removes friction. 2-month runway before first sale expected.
Founder time pressure from W-2 jobs MEDIUM MEDIUM Async-first design: recorded modules, Kajabi automation. 10–14 hrs/week per founder is the ceiling — tracked monthly. Cohort size is the primary lever if time pressure increases.
"This looks like a retail playbook" objection HIGH MEDIUM Every module carries transferability callouts. Module 08 has Build Your Own worksheet. Examples span retail, cybersecurity, identity, and infrastructure. Natalie's Expel/Okta/LastPass IP is co-equal throughout.
Agent quality risk — bad outputs damage partner trust LOW HIGH Human-approval-first for all outputs in first 90 days. Agents unlock after Month 3 only. Liability disclaimer in MSA. The training program is the prerequisite — not a gate, a genuine quality requirement.
Large competitor enters the niche LOW HIGH Build case study library and alumni network fast. Practitioner credibility cannot be replicated. Sandler franchises move on 18–24 month product cycles. First-mover advantage in a narrow niche is meaningful.
