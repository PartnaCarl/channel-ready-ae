A system you run once a week is worth more than a technique you reach for when things get hard.

Every framework in the The RELAY Framework™ works in isolation. I/R Theory helps you stay composed in a tough partner negotiation. The CAA produces clarity in a QBR that was drifting. NRS cleans up a stalled partner relationship. But the real leverage comes when all six frameworks are running simultaneously, as a system — where the CAA opens every partner conversation automatically, the Partner Fit Filter runs in every co-sell call by default, and the No-Free-Consulting standard is so clear that partners never test it.

This module is about moving from deliberate application to automatic execution. That shift requires three things: a weekly operating rhythm that embeds the frameworks into your existing workflow, a partner tiering system built on The Multiplier Method qualification criteria, and a quarterly self-assessment that catches drift before it becomes habit.

The Core Insight

You cannot apply The Multiplier Method in a difficult partner conversation if you haven't been practicing it in easy ones. The system builds the habit. The habit builds the composure. The composure produces the results.

Three operating rhythms. Weekly, monthly, quarterly.

Weekly Rhythm

Pipeline review + CAA prep

Every Friday: review every deal in your pipeline against the Anatomy of a Partner Deal checklist. For every deal not past its Stage checkpoint, set a CAA for the following week's partner conversation. CAA prep takes 5 minutes per deal and saves hours of unproductive follow-up.

Monthly Rhythm

Partner portfolio scorecard

First Monday of each month: score every active partner on four criteria — deals registered in the last 30 days, CAA completion rate, Partner Fit Filter diagnosis documented, No-Free-Consulting compliance. Partners who score below threshold get a NRS conversation before the next month's check-in.

Quarterly Rhythm

The Multiplier Method QBR + self-assessment

Run the The Multiplier Method QBR framework with every partner. And run it on yourself: where did I give away free consulting this quarter? Where did I skip the CAA because it felt unnecessary? Where did I let a partner stall without running NRS? The self-assessment is the most important QBR you'll run.

Partner tiering through The Multiplier Method qualification criteria.

Traditional partner tiering uses revenue as the primary criterion: Gold, Silver, Bronze — ranked by how much they've sold. The Multiplier Method partner tiering uses qualification as the primary criterion: how well has this partner been qualified against must-have criteria, and how consistently are they demonstrating commitment behaviors?

Tier 1 — Qualified and Active

Full The Multiplier Method engagement

Has cleared the Partner Fit Filter qualification. Consistently uses UFCs before partner calls. Registers deals before requesting co-sell support. Attends QBRs prepared and leaves with a commitment list. These are your 20% who produce 80% of your partner revenue.

Tier 2 — Qualified but Inconsistent

Partial The Multiplier Method engagement

Has the right customer base and demonstrated some wins. Inconsistent on deal registration and CAA compliance. Not yet in the No-Free-Consulting discipline. Investment: 1-2 NRS conversations and a tighter co-sell support standard.

Tier 3 — Unqualified or Passive

No The Multiplier Method engagement

Has not cleared qualification criteria or has been active for 90+ days without producing a qualified deal. Investment: one NRS conversation. Outcome: re-engage with commitment or exit cleanly.

The Quarterly Self-Assessment — Six Questions

1. Did I establish a CAA before every partner QBR this quarter?
2. Did I run the Partner Fit Filter before investing time in any co-sell engagement?
3. Did I enforce No-Free-Consulting on every Tier 2 and 3 partner?
4. Did I run NRS on every partner who stalled for more than 30 days?
5. Did I apply DISC profiling in my top five partner relationships?
6. Did I leave every QBR with a named commitment list?

The Multiplier Method Certification — what it means and what it earns you.

Completing all 12 modules and passing the The Multiplier Method Posture Score assessment qualifies you for the The Multiplier Method certificate — a practitioner credential demonstrating fluency in six RELAY frameworks as applied to the indirect channel.

The certificate is not a participation trophy. It represents a documented baseline: you have been exposed to each framework, practiced each application scenario, and self-assessed your posture against each The Multiplier Method discipline. That baseline is what makes the credential meaningful as a differentiator — and what makes the biennial renewal requirement defensible. The channel changes. The frameworks stay the same. Your fluency needs maintenance.

What the Certification Represents

An AE who holds the The Multiplier Method certificate can answer this question in any partner conversation: "Which The Multiplier Method framework applies right now, and what does applying it look like in this specific situation?" That capability — methodological fluency under pressure — is what separates a channel AE with a system from one who is improvising.
