The Capstone

Eleven modules. One operating system. Repeatable revenue.

This module doesn't introduce new techniques. It integrates the eleven modules you've completed into a daily, weekly, and quarterly system — one that compounds over time instead of fading after the training ends.

Most sales training produces a temporary performance spike followed by reversion to old habits. The skills feel powerful in the classroom and awkward in the field. Two months later, the old behaviors are back. This happens because the training was never converted into a system — a repeatable set of habits and cadences that run automatically, without requiring conscious effort every time.

The Sandler system is designed for exactly this kind of institutionalization. The UFC, the Pain Funnel, the stage-gate deal model, the weekly co-prospecting cadence, the Sandler QBR — each one is a discipline that becomes a habit through repetition. This module maps out the daily, weekly, and quarterly cadence that converts everything you've learned into a professional channel operating system.

THE CORE INSIGHT
A skill practiced occasionally is a technique. A skill practiced daily becomes a habit. A habit built into a system becomes a competitive advantage that compounds quarterly. The goal of this program is not to teach you eleven techniques — it's to install eleven habits.

The Sandler Channel Operating System

Daily, weekly, and quarterly cadence that systematizes all 11 modules.

Daily Habits — The Sandler Channel AE Morning Stack

DAILY HABIT STACK
Before your first partner call: I/R check (Module 01) — "What am I afraid of today? Name it. Separate it from my identity."

For every partner meeting: Open with a UFC (Module 03). No exceptions. Even a 10-minute call gets a stated purpose and outcome.

For every deal conversation: Check the stage gate (Module 08). What stage is this deal in? What is the checkpoint? What is missing?

For every inbound request from a partner: Apply No-Free-Consulting (Module 07). What are they asking for? What am I asking in return?

End of day: Log any new competitive intelligence heard (Module 11). One sentence per insight. Builds into your quarterly competitive brief.

Weekly Cadence — The Partner Pipeline Rhythm

MON
Monday — Pipeline Review
  • Review all active deals against stage-gate criteria
  • Flag anything that hasn't moved in 10+ days
  • Apply NRS or diagnostic question to stalled deals
  • Confirm next step exists for every deal in pipeline
WED
Wednesday — Co-Prospect Calls
  • 30-min co-prospect call with each active partner
  • Review target account list: what went out, what came back
  • Agree on next outreach actions and owner
  • Flag any partner going passive — apply NRS if needed
FRI
Friday — Forecast Hygiene
  • Update deal stages based on the week's activity
  • Remove any deals that failed the stage-gate diagnostic
  • Confirm QBR schedule and pre-send materials for upcoming reviews
  • Log week's competitive intelligence observations

Quarterly System — The Sandler Channel Cycle

Quarter Start (Week 1–2)

Run QBRs with top partners using Module 09 framework. Build commitment lists. Update target account lists with partners using Module 10 joint prospecting model. Review competitive brief using Module 11 framework.

Quarter Mid-Point (Week 6–7)

Run monthly syncs with partners. Review commitment list from QBR. Apply NRS to any partner who has drifted (Module 06). Run Pain Funnel on any stalled deals (Module 04). Add new targets to joint prospecting list.

Quarter End (Week 11–12)

Prepare QBR data for upcoming quarter review. Close or remove all pipeline that doesn't meet stage-gate criteria. Run DISC review of key partner contacts — has anything changed? Update partner qualification status for any partner who has gone inactive 90+ days.

Between Quarters

Review which modules produced the most behavioral change. Identify which habits have become automatic and which still require conscious effort. Set a focus module for the next quarter — the one where you still default to old behavior under pressure.

Building Your Personal Channel OS

The configuration decisions that make the system yours — not generic.

A system that isn't personalized won't get used. The Sandler Channel Operating System needs to be configured to your specific territory: your partner mix, your deal cycle length, your product's typical pain pattern, and your natural DISC style. This section walks you through the four configuration decisions that make the system fit your reality.

Configuration 1 — Partner Tier Assignment

Not every partner gets the same level of system investment. Tier your partners based on their production potential and current activity level, then match the cadence intensity to the tier.

Tier 1 — Strategic Partners

Full weekly co-prospect cadence. Formal QBR every quarter. Monthly sync. Named accounts list actively managed. NFC enforced. All 11 module skills applied in full.

Tier 2 — Active Partners

Bi-weekly co-prospect touch. Informal QBR quarterly. Stage-gate review monthly. Target account list reviewed quarterly. Core Sandler skills applied but lighter cadence.

Tier 3 — Developing Partners

Monthly check-in. Pipeline reviewed when deals are active. UFC applied in all meetings. Qualification gate enforced — if no pipeline by 90 days, downgrade or exit using Module 02 disqualification framework.

Configuration 2 — Your Natural DISC Style Applied

Knowing your own DISC profile (Module 05) tells you where the system will run easily and where you'll need to push against your defaults. If you're a natural D, the Pain Funnel (Module 04) will feel uncomfortably slow — build in reminders to hold the silence and stay curious. If you're a natural I, the stage-gate discipline (Module 08) will feel bureaucratic — build in a pipeline review with a colleague to keep you honest. Build your personal system around your natural weaknesses, not just your strengths.

Configuration 3 — The Sandler Trigger List

YOUR PERSONAL TRIGGER LIST
Document the situations where you most often default to old, non-Sandler behavior. Examples:

"When a partner goes quiet for 10+ days, I [used to] follow up with a discount offer. Now I [apply NRS from Module 06]."

"When a partner asks me to write a proposal before qualifying the customer, I [used to] write the proposal. Now I [apply NFC from Module 07 and require the economic buyer to attend the proposal meeting]."

"When I feel like a partner is losing interest, I [used to] add more value for free. Now I [recognize the Not-OK signal from Module 01 and pause my investment]."

This list becomes your personal Sandler manual. Review it before every partner call in your first 90 days.

The 90-Day Post-Program Commitment

The skills from this program will compound or they will fade. The difference is whether you commit to the system for 90 days after completion. Specifically: use the UFC in every partner meeting for 90 days. Run the weekly co-prospect cadence with at least two partners for 90 days. Apply the stage-gate diagnostic to your pipeline every Monday for 90 days. At the 90-day mark, measure: how has your pipeline quality changed? How has your partner engagement changed? How has your forecast accuracy changed? The answers will tell you whether the habits have taken hold.

CLOSING THOUGHT
The Sandler system is not a closing technique. It's not a script. It's a way of operating in every professional relationship — as a peer, with a secure identity, with clear mutual agreements, with genuine curiosity about pain, and with the discipline to walk away when the fit isn't right. That operating posture, applied consistently in channel sales, is what separates the AEs who get mindshare from the ones who beg for it.
