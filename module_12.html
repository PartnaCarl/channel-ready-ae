A system you run once a week is worth more than a technique you reach for when things get hard.

Every framework in The RELAY Framework™ works in isolation. I/R Theory helps you stay composed in a tough partner negotiation. The CAA produces clarity in a QBR that was drifting. NRS cleans up a stalled partner relationship. But the real leverage comes when all six frameworks are running simultaneously, as a system — where the CAA opens every partner conversation automatically, the Partner Fit Filter runs in every co-sell call by default, and the No-Free-Consulting standard is so clear that partners never test it.

This module is about moving from deliberate application to automatic execution. That shift requires three things: a weekly operating rhythm that embeds the frameworks into your existing workflow, a partner tiering system built on The Multiplier Method qualification criteria, and a quarterly self-assessment that catches drift before it becomes habit.

The Core Insight

You cannot apply The Multiplier Method in a difficult partner conversation if you haven't been practicing it in easy ones. The system builds the habit. The habit builds the composure. The composure produces the results.

Three operating rhythms. Weekly, monthly, quarterly.

Weekly Rhythm

Pipeline review + CAA prep

Every Friday: review every deal in your pipeline against the Anatomy of a Partner Deal checklist. For every deal not past its Stage checkpoint, set a CAA for the following week's partner conversation. CAA prep takes 5 minutes per deal and saves hours of unproductive follow-up.

Monthly Rhythm

Partner portfolio scorecard

First Monday of each month: score every active partner on four criteria — deals registered in the last 30 days, CAA completion rate, Partner Fit Filter diagnosis documented, No-Free-Consulting compliance. Partners who score below threshold get a NRS conversation before the next month's check-in.

Quarterly Rhythm

The Multiplier Method QBR + self-assessment

Run The Multiplier Method QBR framework with every partner. And run it on yourself: where did I give away free consulting this quarter? Where did I skip the CAA because it felt unnecessary? Where did I let a partner stall without running NRS? The self-assessment is the most important QBR you'll run.

Partner tiering through The Multiplier Method qualification criteria.

Traditional partner tiering uses revenue as the primary criterion: Gold, Silver, Bronze — ranked by how much they've sold. The Multiplier Method partner tiering uses qualification as the primary criterion: how well has this partner been qualified against must-have criteria, and how consistently are they demonstrating commitment behaviors?

Tier 1 — Qualified and Active

Full The Multiplier Method engagement

Has cleared the Partner Fit Filter qualification. Consistently uses UFCs before partner calls. Registers deals before requesting co-sell support. Attends QBRs prepared and leaves with a commitment list. These are your 20% who produce 80% of your partner revenue.

Tier 2 — Qualified but Inconsistent

Partial The Multiplier Method engagement

Has the right customer base and demonstrated some wins. Inconsistent on deal registration and CAA compliance. Not yet in the No-Free-Consulting discipline. Investment: 1-2 NRS conversations and a tighter co-sell support standard.

Tier 3 — Unqualified or Passive

No The Multiplier Method engagement

Has not cleared qualification criteria or has been active for 90+ days without producing a qualified deal. Investment: one NRS conversation. Outcome: re-engage with commitment or exit cleanly.

The Quarterly Self-Assessment — Six Questions

1. Did I establish a CAA before every partner QBR this quarter?
2. Did I run the Partner Fit Filter before investing time in any co-sell engagement?
3. Did I enforce No-Free-Consulting on every Tier 2 and 3 partner?
4. Did I run NRS on every partner who stalled for more than 30 days?
5. Did I apply DISC profiling in my top five partner relationships?
6. Did I leave every QBR with a named commitment list?

The 120-Day Ramp Model — Template for Every New Partner or Territory

Whether launching a new partnership or opening a new territory, use this 120-day framework to build momentum systematically instead of hoping for organic traction.

Days 1–30
Foundation
Identify champion reps, run initial account mapping, deliver first enablement session, bring one live deal to co-work.
Days 20–45
Activation
Launch 1:4 Blitz in top regions, establish weekly cadence with champion reps, run first joint prospecting calls.
Days 45–60
Proof Points
Close or advance first co-sell deals, capture win stories, present initial heatmap to partner leadership.
Days 60–90
Scale
Expand to second-tier regions based on heatmap, run first formal QBR, establish incentive framework.
Days 90–120
Systematize
Lock in quarterly QBR rhythm, formalize Top 20 tracking, set next-quarter commitments, transition from ramp to operating cadence.
Why 120 Days

30 days to prove you're serious. 60 days to prove you deliver. 90 days to prove it scales. 120 days to make it permanent. Most vendor-partner relationships die in the first 60 days because the vendor stops showing up after the first enablement session.

The Executive Event Playbook

Intimate executive events — executive roundtables (CISO, CIO, VP of Infrastructure), leadership dinners — are force multipliers for partner relationships. They create access you can't get through normal channels.

Format

8–12 attendees, in-person

Small enough for real conversation. Mix partner executives, customer decision-makers (CISOs, CIOs, VPs of Infrastructure), and one or two industry voices. No vendor presentations.

Structure

Partner co-hosts

Your top partner co-hosts and co-invites. They get credit for the relationship access. You get positioned as the vendor who creates value beyond product.

Content

NOT a product pitch

Facilitated discussion on a topic the room cares about — threat landscape, compliance changes, operational efficiency, board-level metrics. Your product is mentioned only if asked.

Success Metric

Target: 3 qualified pipeline opportunities within 30 days of each event. If you're not generating pipeline from executive events, your format or invite list needs work.

KPI Refinement — Replace Vanity Metrics

The channel defaults to counting activity because activity is easy to count. Replace vanity metrics with indicators that actually predict revenue.

Replace

Total reps trained

With: Reps who sourced a deal within 30 days of training. Training without pipeline creation is entertainment, not enablement.

Replace

Partners onboarded

With: Partners with active pipeline at 90 days. Onboarding without activation is a sunk cost.

Add

Assessment-to-opportunity conversion

Track: What percentage of channel assessments convert to qualified pipeline? This is the single best leading indicator for advisory partner motions.

Stop reporting "deals registered" as success. Report "deals registered that progressed past Stage 2." Registration without progression is just paperwork.

The Multiplier Method Certification — what it means and what it earns you.

Completing all 12 modules and passing The Multiplier Method Posture Score assessment qualifies you for The Multiplier Method certificate — a practitioner credential demonstrating fluency in six RELAY frameworks as applied to the indirect channel.

The certificate is not a participation trophy. Your fluency needs maintenance.

That capability — methodological fluency under pressure — is what separates a channel AE with a system from one who is improvising.
