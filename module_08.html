Why This Module Exists

A partner rep who sells your widget once has no reason to come back.

The 3-tier play model solves this by giving every rep a clear expansion path. Tier 1 gets you in the door. Tier 2 adds services and integration. Tier 3 becomes a strategic platform commitment. Each tier is bigger, stickier, and more profitable for the partner.

Workshop

Build your own 3-tier play structure in one sitting.

Step 1 — Name Your Tiers

What are the 3 versions of a deal with your product? Entry/Growth/Enterprise? Refresh/Modernize/Transform? Basic/Pro/Platform? Name them in language a partner rep can remember in 5 seconds.

Step 2 — Write the Signals

"TIER 1 IF: [signal]." Write 3 qualification signals per tier. These tell the rep when to call you — which is more important than teaching them your features.

Step 3 — Map the Pull-Through

What services does the partner sell alongside each tier? This is the single most important exercise. No pull-through = no partner motivation = no deals beyond Tier 1.

THE MOST IMPORTANT FAQ
"What if the partner rep has never sold my category before?"

Answer: Start with Tier 1 as an infrastructure conversation. The rep is not expected to be an expert in your domain. They're expected to open a discovery conversation using the qualification signals you gave them. Your SE or overlay supports the technical depth. The rep's job is the relationship and the intro. Make that clear and they'll try.
