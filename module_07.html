Why This Module Exists

Incentives are the engine. Everything else is fuel.

Most startup AEs don't know this is how the channel economy works. Partner reps are independent operators maximizing their own compensation across hundreds of vendor relationships. They act on the ones that reward specific behaviors with specific dollar amounts at specific times.

THE RULE NOBODY TELLS YOU
Always announce the incentive at the end of the training call, not the beginning. Starting with "you'll get $25 for this" signals the content isn't worth their time. Ending with "and for showing up today..." rewards action already taken. The reveal is a gift, not a bribe.

Startup Budgets

How to build an incentive program on real startup money.

$5,000 Budget

$25/training × 50 reps = $1,250
$100/demo × 20 = $2,000
$300/deal reg × 5 = $1,500
Reserve $250 buffer

75 partner rep interactions for $5K. Enough to activate one major partner.

$1,000 Budget

Skip training incentive.
$200/customer meeting × 3 = $600
$100/demo × 4 = $400

Fewer incentives, higher threshold, still creates action. "First 3 customer meetings get $200 each."

Non-Cash That Works

Amazon gift cards, AirPods, Yeti coolers, dinner reservations. A $75 Amazon card delivered in 48 hours beats a $200 check that takes 60 days to process. Speed of delivery matters as much as amount.

Enterprise Reference: Toshiba × CDW

$50K total SPIF budget. $500 per qualified deal registration. Target: ~100 registrations. This is what a fully funded partner incentive program looks like at scale — your $5K version uses the same ladder architecture.
