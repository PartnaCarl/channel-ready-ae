Why This Module Exists

Your partner grades you every month. Here's the rubric.

Partners don't announce when they're deprioritizing you. They just stop returning calls. The Six Asks framework gives you a self-assessment rubric so you catch the problem before it's too late.

This framework was formalized from Carl's CDW & HPE Asks tracking — a monthly scorecard that rated vendor performance across six dimensions. Natalie validated it from the reseller side at CDW and built a similar partner satisfaction framework at Expel. The six asks are universal: every VAR, in every vertical, evaluates vendors on these same dimensions.

NATALIE'S INSIGHT
The #1 partner complaint is not pricing or product. It's inconsistent engagement. Vendors who show up for 3 weeks, disappear for 6, then reappear asking "any pipeline?" are the ones who get cut from the QBR agenda. Consistency beats brilliance in channel sales.
The Framework

The Six Things Every Partner Evaluates You On.

📝
1. Deal Reg & Pricing
  • Deal registration turnaround under 48 hours Standard
  • Competitive pricing that protects partner margin Margin
  • Clear, simple deal reg portal — no 15-field forms Process
  • Conflict resolution SLA: 72 hours max Escalation

If your deal reg process is slow or confusing, partners submit to the vendor who makes it easy. This is the most transactional ask — and the one where failure is most visible.

💬
2. Quoting & Selling
  • Accurate, fast quotes — same day or next day Speed
  • Pre-built configurations for common use cases Ease
  • Battle cards the rep can use without reading a 40-page deck Assets
  • FAQ doc answering "what if I've never sold this before?" Onboard

If a partner rep can't quote your product in 10 minutes, they'll quote the vendor they can.

📅
3. Consistent Proactive Engagement
  • Weekly or bi-weekly co-sell sessions — non-negotiable Cadence
  • Monthly DM syncs with data, not just conversation Management
  • Don't go dark for 6 weeks then reappear asking for pipeline Consistency
  • Respond to partner emails within 4 business hours Responsiveness

This is the ask that matters most. Show up, every time, on schedule. Consistency is the foundation of partner trust.

🔄
4. Own the Customer Lifecycle
  • Handle your own renewals and support escalations Ownership
  • Don't dump post-sale problems on the partner Responsibility
  • Proactively communicate renewal timelines to the partner Visibility
  • Share customer health data with the partner AM Transparency

Partners who get blamed for your product's support failures will stop selling it. Own your customer experience end-to-end.

🗺️
5. Product Roadmaps
  • Share where your product is going — not just where it is Vision
  • Give partners 1-quarter advance notice on major releases Timing
  • Position upcoming features as co-sell opportunities Revenue
  • Never surprise a partner with a pricing change Trust
👥
6. Organizational Changes
  • When your team changes, communicate immediately Urgency
  • Introduce the replacement before the predecessor leaves Transition
  • New contact inherits the relationship context, not just the name Continuity
  • #1 cause of partner decay: vendor contact disappears without notice Risk
Self-Assessment

Monthly RAG scorecard — grade yourself honestly.

Score yourself against each Ask every month: Green (meeting expectations), Amber (slipping), Red (failing). Share this scorecard with your manager. Use it in QBR prep.

THE WARNING SIGNAL
If you have two or more Reds for two consecutive months, the partner is deprioritizing you — guaranteed. They won't tell you. They'll just stop responding as quickly, stop bringing you into deals, and start routing opportunities to the vendor who's Green across the board. The scorecard catches the slide before it's irreversible.

Operational Rhythm

Monthly maintenance checklist — keep all six asks green.

Deal Reg & Pricing
Review all pending deal registrations — none older than 48 hours unprocessed
BLOCKING. A deal reg sitting in queue for a week is a partner who's losing trust.
Check for deal reg conflicts — resolve or escalate any flagged submissions
Unresolved conflicts within 72 hours become partner relationship problems.
Engagement & Communication
Confirm all scheduled co-sell sessions were held this month (target: 4/month)
If you cancelled more than one, you're sliding toward Amber on Ask #3.
Send Win Wire to partner DM with this month's numbers
Even if the numbers are thin. Consistent reporting builds credibility.
Review email response time — were all partner emails answered within 4 hours?
If you have unanswered partner emails from this week, fix it before checking this box.
Enablement & Assets
Confirm battle card is current — no outdated pricing, features, or competitive claims
Review quarterly at minimum. Update immediately after any product or pricing change.
Check if any product updates need to be communicated to partner reps
Ask #5 — roadmap visibility. One email, one paragraph, one reason it matters to their customers.
RAG Assessment
Complete the monthly RAG scorecard for this partner
5 minutes. Last Friday of the month. Non-negotiable.
If any Ask is Red, write a 1-sentence corrective action plan

The emails that maintain the rhythm.

I'll bring the accounts and a quick update on [product news]. [Your Name] P.S. — the $[X] SPIF program is still active. I'll have the updated details for you on our call.
