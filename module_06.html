Why This Module Exists

Without a co-sell session, your partner relationship is a social club.

Co-selling is the weekly operational rhythm that turns partner goodwill into named accounts with next steps. The Two-Spreadsheet Model gives both sides shared visibility into what's being worked, who owns it, and what happens next.

Spreadsheet 1 — Your Pipeline

Every active opportunity where the partner has a relationship. Columns: Account, your deal stage, partner AM, relationship status, next action, date. This is the "work together" list.

Spreadsheet 2 — Unattached Prospects

Accounts in your territory with no partner assigned, mapped to partner AMs. These are net-new-logo opportunities. Columns: Account, why it's a fit, partner AM in territory, the ask (intro, joint call, demo).

THE 4:1 RULE
Schedule 4 co-sell sessions for every 1 QBR. Weekly sessions are the engine. Monthly DM syncs are management. Quarterly QBRs are formal review. If you only meet quarterly, you don't have a co-sell motion — you have a reporting obligation.
The Operating Rhythm

30 minutes. Every week. Same time.

5MIN

Mini-Enablement

One "reason to call" for the week. A customer profile, a competitive talking point, or a new feature. Keep it under 5 minutes and tied to a specific customer type the rep will see this week.

15MIN

Spreadsheet 1 Review

Status updates on active deals. Which moved forward? Which stalled? Where do you need the partner's help? End each account discussion with a named next step, named owner, and specific date.

10MIN

Spreadsheet 2 Review

New accounts to pursue. Which unattached prospects should the partner introduce? Assign ownership. Set first-touch dates. Goal: 2–3 new accounts assigned per session.

"Let's circle back" is not a next step. Every account discussed leaves the session with a named action, a named person, and a date. If the rep says "I'll look into it," reply: "Great — can we say by Friday? I'll follow up if I don't hear."
Setup

Co-sell campaign setup checklist.

Campaign Foundation
Build Spreadsheet 1: your active pipeline with partner relationship flags
Pull every open opportunity, cross-reference against the partner's territory/account list.
Build Spreadsheet 2: unattached prospects mapped to partner AMs by territory
These are your net-new opportunities. 20–30 accounts is a good starting list.
Schedule recurring weekly co-sell session — 30 min, same day/time
Recurring calendar invite. The consistency matters more than the day.
Share both spreadsheets with partner contacts before first session
"Same info both ways" — they see what you see. No information asymmetry.
Prepare 5-minute mini-enablement for the first session
One customer profile, one qualification signal, one reason to call. Keep it tight.
