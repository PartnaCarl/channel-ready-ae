Buyer FAQ

FAQs

Is this officially licensed by Sandler Training?
No. We've adapted The Multiplier Method's publicly documented methodology — Partner Fit Filter, CAA, NRS, DISC, I/R Theory, No-Free-Consulting — specifically for the indirect channel. The frameworks are The Multiplier Method's. The channel application, scenarios, and curriculum are ours. Reviewed by legal counsel.
My partner is Optiv, not CDW. Does this transfer?
RELAY framework stages are structurally identical across partner types. Whether you work with Value-Added Resellers (VARs), Managed Service Providers (MSPs), distributors, or Independent Software Vendors (ISVs) — the Partner Fit Filter qualifies the same way, the CAA runs the same way. Scenarios are drawn from multiple partner types, including Managed Security Service Provider (MSSP), Systems Integrator (SI), national VAR, and regional reseller.
Do my AEs need The Multiplier Method training first?
No prior The Multiplier Method training required. Each framework is introduced from first principles and applied immediately to partner situations. Direct-sales The Multiplier Method experience is helpful but not a prerequisite. The program is designed to be the first place your AEs encounter The Multiplier Method.
What is the Channel Strategy Audit and how does it connect to the curriculum?
The audit is a 2-hour working session ($2,500 flat) where Carl or Natalie diagnoses your partner motion and delivers a written action plan. The action plan maps directly to curriculum modules — so teams that go through an audit know exactly which Phase and Module addresses each gap. It's designed as the top of the funnel, not a standalone product.
What does the certificate mean?
The Multiplier Method certificate demonstrates practitioner-level fluency in six RELAY frameworks as applied to the indirect channel. It's a credential that differentiates AEs who can articulate their channel methodology from those who can't — useful in performance reviews, job searches, and conversations with partner leadership about program maturity.
Why This Program Exists

This program exists because the two people who built it have sat on opposite sides of every channel deal.

Carl spent 15+ years on the vendor side — building partner programs at HPE, LastPass, Mimecast, and Toshiba, running co-sell motions across CDW, SHI, Insight, WWT, and nine other national VARs. Natalie spent her career on the reseller and partner side — at CDW, then across six security and cloud software companies, running the channel programs that vendors were trying to activate.

Neither side teaches the other what it's like to be across the table. Vendors don't know why partners deprioritize them. Partners don't know what it takes to make a vendor program worth selling. This curriculum is the first one built from both seats simultaneously — and that's the only reason it covers what other channel training doesn't.
