The The RELAY Framework™

Six frameworks. All adapted for channel.

The Multiplier Method's core frameworks were built for direct sales. We've adapted each one specifically for the indirect channel — where the buyer is a partner, not an end customer — into a proprietary methodology we call The The RELAY Framework™.

Framework 01

Identity / Role Theory

Channel AEs who over-invest emotionally in partner relationships give away the most. I/R Theory separates your identity from your role — so partner rejection doesn't trigger free consulting behavior.

Framework 02

Partner Fit Filter

Applied to both partner qualification and end-customer conversations run through partners. Uncovers real pain — not feature interest — before any investment of time, travel, or IP.

Framework 03

Co-Sell Alignment Agreements

Every partner conversation — intro call, QBR, joint sales call — begins with a CAA. Agenda, purpose, outcomes, and next steps agreed before the meeting starts. Every time.

Framework 04

Negative Reverse Selling

Used when partners stall, go quiet, or ask for more resources without commitment. NRS surfaces the real objection — or disqualifies the partner — without breaking the relationship.

Framework 05

No-Free-Consulting

The framework for protecting your time, your IP, and your deal margins. Defines exactly what requires partner commitment before you engage — and what happens when they take your work to a competitor.

Framework 06

DISC for Channel

Reading partner personality types and adapting communication style accordingly. D-types need decisions, not rapport. C-types need data, not enthusiasm. The wrong approach with the right partner is still a lost deal.
