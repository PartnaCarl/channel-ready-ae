The TRACE Framework™

Five TRACE stages. All adapted for channel.

The Multiplier Method's core frameworks were built from first principles for the indirect channel — where the buyer is a partner, not an end customer. The proprietary five-stage framework: Target, Register, Align, Co-sell, Expand. Every module in the curriculum is anchored to one or more TRACE stages.

Framework 01

Identity / Role (I/R) Theory

Channel Account Executives who over-invest emotionally in partner relationships give away the most. I/R Theory separates your identity from your role — so partner rejection doesn't trigger free consulting behavior.

For VPs: stops the root behavior that inflates pipeline and destroys margin — giving away IP before commitment.
Framework 02

Partner Fit Filter

Applied to both partner qualification and end-customer conversations run through partners. Uncovers real pain — not feature interest — before any investment of time, travel, or Intellectual Property.

For VPs: eliminates the hours your AEs spend on unqualified partners who appear in pipeline reviews at Stage 3 with no budget and no champion.
Framework 03

Co-Sell Alignment Agreements

Every partner conversation — intro call, Quarterly Business Review, joint sales call — begins with a Co-Sell Alignment Agreement. Agenda, purpose, outcomes, and next steps agreed before the meeting starts. Every time.

For VPs: ends the 'we're aligned' problem — partner calls that produce no agreed next step, no commitment, and no forward motion.
Framework 04

Negative Reverse Selling

Used when partners stall, go quiet, or ask for more resources without commitment. Negative Reverse Selling surfaces the real objection — or disqualifies the partner — without breaking the relationship.

For VPs: removes the partners inflating your pipeline with 90+ days of 'activity' and no qualified deal attached.
Framework 05

No-Free-Consulting

The framework for protecting your time, your Intellectual Property, and your deal margins. Defines exactly what requires partner commitment before you engage — and what happens when they take your work to a competitor.

For VPs: directly protects margin. If your AEs are building proposals and architectures for partners who take that work to a cheaper vendor — this framework stops it.
Framework 06

DISC for Channel

Reading partner personality types and adapting communication style accordingly. D-types need decisions, not rapport. C-types need data, not enthusiasm. The wrong approach with the right partner is still a lost deal.

For VPs: solves the stuck relationship problem — where a key partner hasn't produced in months and nobody can explain why.
The TRACE Framework™ — One Deal, Start to Finish

What it looks like when the whole system runs.

Below is a complete deal walkthrough — one channel AE, one regional VAR, one end customer. Every TRACE framework in this curriculum appears in the order you'd use it in the field. Read this before Module 1. Read it again after Module 12.

The Scenario

Sarah is a channel AE at a mid-market cybersecurity vendor. Her partner is Apex Networks — a 12-person regional VAR in Columbus, Ohio, focused on manufacturing and logistics. She has had two intro calls. David is Apex's owner. Marcus is the IT Director at Varris Manufacturing, Apex's customer. The deal below is the first real co-sell opportunity they work together.

Partner Fit Filter
Module 02
Stage 1
Qualifying Apex before investing anything

Before her third call with David, Sarah runs the four Must-Have criteria: customer fit (yes — manufacturing fits the ICP), motivation to sell (unclear), capacity to execute (unknown), commitment level (untested). She decides the third call is a qualifying call, not a relationship call.

What Sarah Said

"David, before I invest more time building out what a partnership looks like — I want to make sure we're right for each other. Can I ask you a few direct questions? If the answers tell me this doesn't fit, I'll say so and we can save both of us time. If they do, we'll have a real foundation to work from."

David named three accounts where customers had expressed interest in better endpoint protection. Motivation and capacity confirmed. Partnership activated.
DISC for Channel
Module 05
Stage 1–2
Reading David as a C-type and adjusting immediately

During the qualifying call, Sarah noticed David had prepared notes, corrected a minor detail in her partner program description, asked about documentation, and went silent when she moved too quickly. Classic C-type signals. She had been showing up with I-type energy — stories, enthusiasm, big-picture vision. David decides on proof, not vision.

The Adjustment — Internal

"I changed my next email to three bullet points, one data reference, one clear ask. His response time dropped from 48 hours to 4 hours."

Co-Sell Alignment Agreement
Module 03
Stage 2
Opening every call with structure David could prepare for

Sarah sent the CAA in the calendar invite 48 hours before every call. David — a C-type — responded better to written agendas than to improvised conversation. By the third call, he was sending his own agenda items back. The CAA became their shared working language.

CAA for the First Co-Sell Call

"Today's purpose: decide if the Varris Manufacturing account is worth pursuing together. Agenda: I'll share three pain signals we see in manufacturing accounts, then you tell me what you're hearing from Marcus. If we agree there's a fit, we set a joint call. If we don't, we move to one of your other accounts. Does that work?"

Partner Fit Filter
Module 04
Stage 3
Running joint discovery on Marcus at Varris Manufacturing

David opened the joint call. Sarah ran the Partner Fit Filter. Marcus described "some endpoint issues" (Level 1). Sarah pushed to Level 2: "What does that cost Varris operationally?" Marcus named a Q4 audit and a compliance gap with a new customer contract. Sarah pushed to Level 3.

The Level 3 Question

"Marcus, if we're still having this conversation in February and nothing has changed on the endpoint side — what does that mean for the March contract renewal?"

Marcus: "It means we probably lose that contract." The deal was real from that moment.
No-Free-Consulting
Module 07
Stage 4
When David asked for a full architecture doc before registering the deal

After the discovery call, David asked for a custom architecture document, a competitive comparison against the incumbent vendor, and a pricing scenario analysis. He had not yet registered the deal. Sarah recognized a Tier 3 request without Tier 3 commitment.

What Sarah Said

"I can build all three of those — and I will, because I think this deal is real. Before I do, I need one thing from you: a registered deal with Marcus's name and a proposed decision date. That's not bureaucracy — it's how I know the work I'm doing is going toward a real opportunity. Can you get that submitted today?"

David submitted the deal registration within two hours. Architecture doc delivered the next day.
Negative Reverse Selling
Module 08
Stage 5
Three weeks of silence after the proposal was delivered

David stopped responding. Two follow-ups went unanswered. Sarah recognized Pattern 3 — the go-dark after a strong meeting. She ran NRS instead of sending a third follow-up.

The NRS Email
Subject: Okay to close this out?

"David — I haven't heard back, and I don't want to keep following up if the timing has shifted on Varris. If this isn't moving forward, just say so — I'll close the file and we can revisit if anything changes. If you want to push this to close before March, let me know and I'll pick it back up today."

David responded in 45 minutes. He had been waiting on Marcus to confirm budget approval. He had it. Close call set for the following week. Deal closed: 3-year agreement signed through Apex Networks.

Total time investment from Sarah's first qualifying call to signed agreement: 14 hours across 8 weeks. No free consulting given. No stalls that weren't addressed. No ambiguity about who owned what. That is what the full TRACE system produces — not isolated technique, but a deal that moves in one direction from qualification to close.

What Would Have Happened Without the System
  • Without Partner Fit Filter: four weeks of enablement before discovering the accounts weren't a real fit.
  • Without DISC: I-type energy applied to a C-type owner — David goes cold and Sarah doesn't know why.
  • Without CAA: the joint discovery call has no structure and no next step.
  • Without No-Free-Consulting: David takes the architecture doc, the competitive analysis, and the pricing model to Apex's preferred vendor.
  • Without NRS: the deal dies in the follow-up queue after a third unanswered email.
