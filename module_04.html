Why This Module Exists

Deal reg is the single most important mechanism in channel sales.

It protects margin, prevents conflict, creates accountability, and generates measurable pipeline. Every function is critical. An AE who treats deal reg as paperwork will lose partner trust, lose deals to conflict, and have no way to forecast partner revenue.

NATALIE'S INSIGHT
"Programmatic deal registration structure that is repeatable and measurable combats high churn rates." At LastPass, Natalie drove systematic deal reg adoption across CDW that contributed to 4× revenue growth in 12 months. The mechanism was not a better product pitch — it was a better deal reg process.

Margin Protection

A registered deal gives the partner 5–15% additional margin. This is the partner's primary financial incentive to register — and your primary lever to surface their pipeline. No deal reg = no margin protection = no reason for the partner to tell you about opportunities.

Conflict Prevention

When two partners claim the same customer, deal reg creates a paper trail. First to register with a qualified opportunity gets priority. Without it, you have two angry partners and no resolution framework.

Pipeline Accountability

Deal regs create a pipeline both sides can see. You know what they're working. They know you're tracking. Bilateral visibility turns a casual relationship into an accountable motion.

Churn Prevention

When partners register consistently, they're invested. When they stop, they're leaving. Track deal reg velocity per partner rep as your leading indicator of relationship health.

Processing

What a clean submission looks like vs. a conflict flag.

✓ Clean Submission — Auto-Approve

Named customer with specific contact. Named partner rep who submitted. Specific product or solution identified. Estimated deal value within reasonable range ($5K–$500K, not $1M on a first deal). Expected close date within 6 months. No existing registration on the account from another partner. Process: approve within 24 hours, notify partner, track in CRM, count toward SPIF.

⚠ Conflict Flag — Hold & Investigate

Existing active registration on the same account from another partner. Same account name with slight variation (subsidiary, division, holding company). Same customer, different product line (may not conflict depending on rules). Same partner, same customer, new submission within 30 days of previous expiry (re-registration attempt). Process: hold, investigate, route to you for resolution with full context. Never auto-approve a flagged deal reg — one wrong call destroys partner trust on both sides.

CONFLICT RESOLUTION FRAMEWORK
Step 1: Identify the registering parties and timestamps.
Step 2: Review the opportunity context — who has a deeper customer relationship?
Step 3: Check for prior activity — has either partner had meetings, demos, or proposals with this customer?
Step 4: Default to first-to-register unless the later registrant has demonstrably stronger customer engagement.
Step 5: Communicate the decision to both partners within 72 hours with reasoning — never ghost the losing party.
Step 6: Document the decision for future reference — conflicts on the same account tend to recur.
Measurement

Track deal reg submissions per partner rep, per month.

Deal reg velocity by rep is the leading indicator of partner engagement. It tells you which reps are active, which are disengaging, and where your co-sell sessions are producing results.

Healthy Benchmark

A partner relationship with 3–5 active reps should produce 8–15 deal registrations per quarter. This assumes weekly co-sell sessions and an active SPIF program. Below this signals disengagement.

High Performer Signal

Above 20 regs/quarter from a single rep signals either a highly active partnership or a rep gaming the SPIF — investigate. If real, this is your champion. Protect them.

Warning Signal

Below 5 regs/quarter across all reps means the partnership is functionally dormant. The co-sell sessions aren't producing. Diagnose: is it your product fit, your engagement, or the rep's territory?

Carl's HPE data: Chrysanna — 210 deal regs over the relationship. Justin — 553. The per-rep variance tells you exactly which reps are engaged and which carry your logo in name only. Track it monthly and share it in your QBR.
Operations

Deal registration operations checklist.

Process Setup (One-Time)
Confirm your deal reg portal is accessible to all partner reps
BLOCKING. Reps can't submit what they can't access. Provision credentials before the first training.
Define your approval SLA: target 24 hours for clean, 72 hours for flagged
Write it down. Tell your partners. Hold yourself to it.
Set up conflict detection rules in your CRM or deal reg system
At minimum: same account name match, same domain match, same contact match.
Create a per-rep tracking spreadsheet or CRM dashboard
Columns: Rep name, # submitted this month, # approved, # in conflict, avg deal size.
Weekly Operations
Review all pending deal registrations — none older than 48 hours
A stale deal reg queue is a trust-destroying signal to your partner.
Resolve or escalate any conflict-flagged submissions
72-hour max. Communicate the decision to both parties with reasoning.
Cross-reference new deal regs against co-sell session action items
Are the accounts discussed in co-sell actually getting registered? If not, why not?
Monthly Review
Update per-rep tracking dashboard
Which reps submitted this month? Which didn't? What changed?
Review deal reg conversion rate: submitted → approved → closed
If approved-to-closed is below 20%, the quality of submissions needs attention.
Include deal reg metrics in your monthly Win Wire and QBR prep
